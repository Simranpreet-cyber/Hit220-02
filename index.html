Student Support Study Hub

A simple web concept designed to help new university students find study tips, weekly planning help, and basic motivation in one place.

Explore Features

Project Overview

This site is a small interactive web application prototype. It focuses on a clear structure, easy navigation, and simple interaction for students who need a clean and stress free place to organise study habits.

The design idea is based on the assumption that many first year students feel overwhelmed, lose track of deadlines, and prefer straightforward digital tools over complicated systems.

Quick Summary

  • Simple and responsive layout
  • Separate HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files
  • Interactive study tip button
  • Expandable FAQ section
  • Written brief included in README

Key Features

Study Tip Generator

A student can click a button and instantly get a practical study tip. This creates a small but useful personalised interaction.

Targeted for Beginners

The website is aimed at students who want simple support without needing to learn a complicated app or dashboard.

Clear Content Structure

The page is organised into sections for overview, persona, hypothesis, features, and FAQs so information is easy to scan.

User Persona

Name: Aisha

Age: 20

Background: First year university student with part time work

Goal: Stay organised and reduce study stress

Frustration: Too many systems, too many deadlines, not enough structure

Assumption and Hypothesis

Assumption: Students are more likely to use a lightweight support page when the design is clean, simple, and directly useful.

Hypothesis: If students are given quick study guidance and a straightforward interface, they will feel more confident and engage more consistently with their weekly planning.
