# HIT226 Sprint 2 Project

## Project Brief
This project is a simple student support website concept designed to demonstrate basic web development skills and user focused design thinking. The purpose of the site is to help beginner university students access simple study support in a clean and manageable format. The design focuses on clarity, ease of use, and light interaction, with the goal of reducing confusion and helping users feel more organised and confident.

## Purpose of Site Personalisation
The purpose of site personalisation in this project is to make the website feel relevant and useful for a specific type of user rather than presenting generic information. The page is written around the needs of a beginner student who may feel stressed, busy, and unsure how to organise their study routine. Personalisation appears through targeted content, a specific student persona, and interactive study tips that respond to user action.

## Likely Users
The likely users are first year or beginner university students who need simple study support. These users may be balancing classes, assessments, and part time work, and they may prefer a clear and uncomplicated interface.

## User Persona
Simranpreet is a 20 year old second year university student. She works part time and sometimes struggles to keep up with deadlines. She wants a simple digital space that helps her feel more organised and less overwhelmed. She prefers straightforward tools and clear instructions rather than complex systems.

## Assumption and Hypothesis
One assumption in this project is that students are more likely to engage with a support website when it is simple, visually clear, and directly useful. The hypothesis is that if students are given quick study guidance and a clean interface with a small amount of interaction, they will feel more confident and engage more consistently with study planning.

## How the Example Works for the User
The website presents a structured overview of the project, the target user, and the design idea. It includes an interactive study tip generator that provides a practical tip when the user clicks a button. It also includes expandable FAQ items to make information easier to browse. This keeps the experience simple while still demonstrating functionality and user focused design.
